Frequently Asked Questions

General Information

What is the OFAG?

The Office of the Federal Auditor General (OFAG) is the supreme audit institution of the country.

What is its primary mission?

To ensure public resources are used efficiently, effectively, and in accordance with the law.

Legislation

What proclamation governs the OFAG?

The OFAG operates under Proclamation No. 669/2009.

Audit Process

How are audits initiated?

Audits are initiated based on an annual plan, risk assessment, and legal mandates.

Public Support

How can I contact the Office for further information or inquiries?

You can find our contact details on the Contact Us page.

Can the public request specific audits?

The Office welcomes public input. While the Auditor General prioritizes audits based on legal mandates, suggestions can be submitted through the "Feedback" section.

How does the Office address public concerns regarding government spending?

We take all public concerns seriously and investigate them as part of our audit process.

Technical And Accessibility Support

I am having trouble accessing a report.

Please contact our support team via the contact page with details of the issue.

Popular Questions

Where can I find the Auditor General's Annual Reports?

You can find them in the "Audit Reports" section of our website.

What is the role of the Office of the Federal Auditor General?

The Office ensures accountability by auditing government entities to verify that public funds are used legally, efficiently, and effectively.

Can the public request specific audits?

Yes, through our feedback section.
